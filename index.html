Comparative Political Scientist studying democratic accountability, political behavior, and political economy.
Professor & Director, Division of Political Studies · CIDE

How does political competition shape democratic accountability, citizen behavior, and the political economy of democratic governance? I answer this question by combining comparative politics, political economy, survey methodology, and Bayesian statistics — a rare combination that allows me to both develop new measurement tools and test substantive theories of democratic politics across Latin America.

Politólogo Comparativista especializado en rendición de cuentas democrática, comportamiento político y economía política.
Profesor y Director, División de Estudios Políticos · CIDE

¿Cómo moldea la competencia política la rendición de cuentas democrática, el comportamiento ciudadano y la economía política de la gobernanza? Respondo esta pregunta combinando política comparada, economía política, metodología de encuestas y estadística bayesiana — una combinación poco usual que me permite desarrollar nuevas herramientas de medición y probar teorías sustantivas sobre la política democrática en América Latina.

Research Programs

Programas de Investigación

I study a single phenomenon from multiple angles: how political competition shapes democratic accountability. To answer this question, I draw on political behavior, political economy, survey methodology, subnational elections, and Bayesian methods. Estudio un solo fenómeno desde múltiples ángulos: cómo la competencia política moldea la rendición de cuentas democrática. Para responder esta pregunta, recurro al comportamiento político, la economía política, la metodología de encuestas, las elecciones subnacionales y los métodos bayesianos.

Book Libro

The Illusion of National Politics

Why do we assume national elections produce nationally uniform outcomes? This book uses 36,000+ municipal elections in Mexico (1997-2024) to show that subnational electoral heterogeneity fundamentally challenges how we understand democratic representation.

¿Por qué asumimos que las elecciones nacionales producen resultados uniformes? Este libro analiza más de 36,000 elecciones municipales en México (1997-2024) para mostrar que la heterogeneidad electoral subnacional desafía la forma en que entendemos la representación democrática.

Measuring Hidden Political Preferences Midiendo Preferencias Políticas Ocultas

New inflated discrete choice models (BZiOP) for detecting masked survey responses in sensitive political contexts. Developing identification results for Political Analysis.

Nuevos modelos de elección discreta inflados (BZiOP) para detectar respuestas enmascaradas en contextos políticos sensibles. Desarrollando resultados de identificación para Political Analysis.

About

Acerca de Mí

My work sits at the intersection of comparative politics, political economy, political behavior, and quantitative methodology. What makes my research distinctive is the combination of substantive questions about democratic politics with the development of new statistical tools to answer them. I am as likely to be writing a formal model of survey response as I am to be analyzing 36,000 municipal elections or designing a field experiment in rural Mexico.


As Director of CIDE's Division of Political Studies, I lead one of Latin America's most productive political science departments. I serve on CIDE's Academic Council, its Board of Directors, and the Academic Evaluation Committee. I advise doctoral and master's students working on democratic politics, political behavior, and quantitative methods.


I grew up in Jiutepec, Morelos — a small city south of Mexico City. I was the first person in my family to attend college. I earned my Ph.D. from the University of Notre Dame (2011), an MA in Economics from the University of Pittsburgh, and a BA in Economics from Tec de Monterrey. Before CIDE, I was a tenured associate professor at San José State University.


Outside academia, I am a competitive long-distance runner. I have raced the Berlin, New York, Chicago, and Boston marathons. I have visited 27 countries and counting.

Mi trabajo se sitúa en la intersección de la política comparada, la economía política, el comportamiento político y la metodología cuantitativa. Lo que distingue mi investigación es la combinación de preguntas sustantivas sobre la política democrática con el desarrollo de nuevas herramientas estadísticas para responderlas. Es igual de probable encontrarme escribiendo un modelo formal de respuesta de encuesta que analizando 36,000 elecciones municipales o diseñando un experimento de campo en el México rural.
